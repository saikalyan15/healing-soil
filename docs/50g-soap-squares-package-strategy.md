# 50g Soap Squares Package Strategy

Working reference for the 50g Soap Squares bulk-order program.

## Summary

The 50g format should not be listed as a normal storefront package. Small runs take too much cutting, wrapping, labeling, handling, and coordination time. The format makes sense when someone wants a meaningful quantity for bulk gifting, resale, hospitality, events, or white labelling.

Confirmed decisions:

- No individual 50g soaps for public sale.
- No standard storefront listing for 4-pack retail packages.
- No half-price pricing.
- Minimum order quantity should start at 25 pieces per soap/variant.
- Travel, loofah, kids/gift pouches, inactive Valentine products, and inactive Rice Rose are excluded.
- Names and copy should be descriptive, not therapeutic or medical.

## Strategy

Use `Soap Squares` as the format name. Do not lead with `50g` in customer-facing package names unless weight disclosure is required in quote or label details.

The customer-facing role of the format:

- Bulk gifting for weddings, events, festivals, and corporate orders.
- Guest-bath soaps for homestays, retreats, boutique stays, and studios.
- Resale or white-label batches for stores, creators, and wellness businesses.
- Sample-size discovery only when the quantity justifies production.

The 100g bar should remain the better everyday value. The 50g format should carry a higher per-gram price because cutting, curing, wrapping, labeling, handling, and packaging do not reduce by half.

Do not produce small ad hoc runs. If someone asks for a few pieces, redirect them to 100g bars or existing travel soaps. If someone asks for 25 or more 50g pieces, treat it as a custom/bulk enquiry.

| Base category | Current 100g price | Straight 50g equivalent | Recommended 50g valuation |
|---|---:|---:|---:|
| Glycerin | Rs. 250 | Rs. 125 | Rs. 150 |
| Goat Milk | Rs. 350 | Rs. 175 | Rs. 200 |
| Shea Butter | Rs. 400 | Rs. 200 | Rs. 225 |

Pricing principles:

- 50g bars are not half-price.
- 50g bars carry a higher per-gram cost because handling and packaging effort stays high.
- 100g bars should remain the better everyday value.
- 50g Soap Squares are priced for bulk/custom work, not as discount miniatures.
- White-label or custom packaging should be quoted separately.

## CDSCO-Cautious Naming And Copy Guardrails

Safe naming and copy language:

- `Soap Squares`
- `Glycerin`
- `Goat Milk`
- `Shea Butter`
- `Daily wash`
- `Light`
- `Creamy`
- `Rich`
- `Fresh`
- `Botanical`
- `Citrus`
- `Herb`
- `Gift Set`
- `Discovery Set`
- `Bulk Order`
- `White Label`
- `Guest Bath`

Avoid front-pack or product copy claims containing:

- `anti-acne`
- `eczema`
- `psoriasis`
- `rash`
- `infection`
- `repair`
- `healing`
- `fairness`
- `brightening`
- `whitening`
- `anti-aging`
- `detox`
- `medicated`
- `clinical`
- `for sensitive skin`

Use selection guidance only as soft supporting copy:

- "A lighter wash"
- "A creamy wash"
- "A richer wash"
- "A way to try different bases"
- "Four small-batch soaps in one set"
- "Available for bulk orders"
- "Custom packaging quoted separately"

## Compliance Notes

Reference sources:

- CDSCO Cosmetics page: https://cdsco.gov.in/opencms/opencms/en/Cosmetics/cosmetics/
- CDSCO Cosmetics Rules, 2020 page: https://cdsco.gov.in/opencms/opencms/en/Acts-and-rules/Cosmetics-Rules/

CDSCO's cosmetics page cites Rule 36 of the Cosmetics Rules, 2020: no cosmetic may purport, claim to purport, or convey any idea that is false or misleading to the intending user.

Final printed packaging should still be reviewed for required label fields, including:

- Net quantity
- Batch number
- Manufacturing details
- MRP
- Ingredient declaration
- Manufacturer/marketer details
- Any required caution, use, storage, or expiry details

## Assumptions

- Rice Rose Goat Milk Soap is inactive and excluded.
- These are bulk/custom offers first, not normal storefront SKUs.
- Minimum order quantity starts at 25 pieces per soap/variant.
- White-label work requires separate review of product naming, label responsibility, claims, ingredients, and packaging before production.
