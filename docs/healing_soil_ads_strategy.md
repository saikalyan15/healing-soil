## 2. Ideal Customer Profile (ICP)

Your buyers are a healthy mix — but they share one core trait: they are actively seeking a natural alternative to commercial soaps, either due to skin sensitivity or a desire to avoid harsh chemicals.

- Age: 25–50, urban
- Gender: All genders
- Location: Bangalore & Goa (city-level targeting)
- Income: Middle to upper-middle class (comfortable spending ₹250–₹500 on a soap bar)

### Psychographics
- Conscious consumers — read ingredient labels
- Interested in wellness, yoga, ayurveda, organic living
- Frustrated with mainstream soaps causing dryness or irritation
- Willing to pay a premium for quality and authenticity
- Gift buyers — look for meaningful, beautiful gifts

### Pain Points (Why They Buy)
- Commercial soaps irritate their skin or the scent is too harsh
- Sensitive skin that reacts to sulfates and artificial fragrances
- Looking for trustworthy, handcrafted alternatives

## 3. Platform Strategy

**Recommendation: Meta Only (Instagram + Facebook)**

With a ₹5,000/month budget, do not spread across platforms. Meta is the right call because:

- Instagram is already your home turf — active presence, warm audience
- Visual handmade products perform exceptionally well on feeds and Reels
- Geo-targeting to Bangalore + Goa is precise and cost-effective
- Click-to-WhatsApp ads are natively supported with zero friction

**What NOT to run right now:**
- Google Ads — too expensive at this budget; different buyer intent
- Snapchat / Pinterest — not the right audience for this geography
- YouTube — requires high-quality video production; not the priority

## 4. Campaign Goal: WhatsApp Conversations

**Click-to-WhatsApp Ads — Your Primary CTA**

Ad → WhatsApp tap → Customer asks a question → You answer → They buy.

At your price point and brand size, sending people to WhatsApp will convert far better than sending them to a website. It feels personal and matches your word-of-mouth DNA.

### ⚠️ Managing the Content-to-Conversation Gap

WhatsApp ads put a real time demand on you. If a customer taps the ad at 10 PM and doesn't hear back until morning, the impulse to buy can pass. Here's how to manage this without being glued to your phone:

- **Set up a WhatsApp Business auto-reply** — a warm greeting that acknowledges the inquiry and sets a response window (e.g., "Hi! Thanks for reaching out to Healing Soil 🌿 We'll get back to you within a few hours. In the meantime, browse our full collection at healingsoil.in/shop")
- **Define your response window** — commit to replying within 2–3 hours during waking hours (say, 8 AM–9 PM). Make this your operating norm before you spend on ads.
- **Use WhatsApp Business quick replies** — pre-save responses to common questions (pricing, ingredients, delivery) so you can reply in seconds, not minutes.
- **Batch your responses** — check at fixed times (e.g., 8 AM, 1 PM, 6 PM, 9 PM) rather than reactively. This is sustainable without sacrificing speed.

The goal: no inquiry should wait more than 3 hours during the day, and every late-night tap should get an auto-reply that keeps them warm.

## 5. Budget Breakdown

| Line Item | Amount |
|---|---|
| Active Ad Spend | ₹4,500 / month |
| Buffer (testing & tweaks) | ₹500 / month |
| Daily Spend | ~₹150 / day |
| Total | ₹5,000 / month |

### ⚠️ The Learning Phase Constraint

₹150/day is a lean but viable entry point. However, Meta's algorithm needs roughly 50 conversion events (WhatsApp taps) per ad set per week to exit the "learning phase" and start optimising effectively. At ₹150/day, this could take 3–4 weeks instead of 1–2.

**How to work around this:**
- Start with **fewer, broader ad sets** rather than splitting budget across many narrow audiences — this concentrates spend and helps the algorithm learn faster
- **Don't make changes to an ad** during the first 7 days. Every edit restarts the learning phase clock
- Accept that Month 1 is a data collection exercise, not a profit exercise
- If you can flex to ₹7,000–₹8,000 in month 2, the algorithm will reward you noticeably faster

## 6. Target Audience Setup

### Core Settings
- Location: Bangalore + Goa (city-level)
- Age: 25–50
- Gender: All

### Interest Targeting — Keep It Simple

> ⚠️ **Don't over-layer interests.** Stacking too many interests (Yoga + Ayurveda + Organic + Sensitive Skin + Eco-friendly) shrinks your audience size so much that Meta's algorithm can't optimise effectively — and your cost per click goes up. Each interest you add filters OUT people who don't have all of them.

**Instead, run two separate broad ad sets:**

| Ad Set | Interests | Why |
|---|---|---|
| Ad Set A — Wellness | Natural skincare, Organic products, Ayurveda | Broader wellness angle |
| Ad Set B — Gifting | Handmade/artisan products, Gift shopping | Captures the gifting buyer |

This keeps each audience large enough for Meta to work with, while still being targeted. You'll also learn which angle performs better.

### Exclusion
- Exclude existing page followers — they already know you. Save the budget for new audiences.

## 8. AI-Powered Creative Workflow

Use AI to enhance your existing product photos. Never generate a soap from scratch — it won't look like yours.

### Step 1: Generate Enhanced Backgrounds with ChatGPT (GPT-4o)

Upload your soap photo and use one of these prompts:

**Angle 1 (No Nasties):**
> "Place this soap bar on a rustic wooden surface surrounded by dried herbs and flowers. Soft natural morning light. Clean, minimal, organic feel. No text."

**Angle 2 (Sensitive Skin):**
> "Place this soap on a white linen cloth near a window with soft diffused light. Calming, gentle, spa-like atmosphere."

**Angle 3 (Gift):**
> "Arrange this soap with kraft paper wrapping and dried lavender on a warm earthy background. Gift-ready styling."

### Step 2: Polish in Canva
- Import the AI-generated image into Canva
- Add ad headline text and your brand name
- Add WhatsApp CTA on the final slide or bottom strip
- Export as 1080x1080 (feed) or 1080x1920 (Stories/Reels)

### AI Toolkit

| Tool | Use Case | Cost |
|---|---|---|
| ChatGPT (GPT-4o) | Background generation, scene styling | Free / Plus |
| Canva | Image ads, text overlays, carousel layouts | Free |
| CapCut | Reel editing, auto-captions, voiceover | Free |
| Remove.bg | Clean up product photo backgrounds | Free |
| Adobe Express | Quick single-image ad polish | Free |

**What AI does well:** Placing your soap in new styled backgrounds, changing surfaces and props, mood and lighting adjustments.

**What AI does NOT do well:** Fine text on soap labels (often gets distorted), exact brand colours. Always use your actual product photo as the base.
