## Individual breakdown

### 1. Nivre Natural

**What they have:**
- Physical Bangalore address (local SEO signal)
- URL keyword "handmade-soap-manufacturers-in-bangalore"
- Sensitive skin collection with named ingredients (oatmeal, honey)
- ShipRocket + Shopify setup

**What they're doing wrong:**
- URL targets "manufacturers" — a B2B keyword. B2C buyers don't search for "manufacturers."
- H1 is "Best Soaps for Sensitive Skin in India" — doesn't match the URL keyword at all. Signals confusion between targeting B2B and B2C.
- No meta description on the collection page.
- Main copy uses CDSCO-violating language: "calm irritation", "hydrate deeply", "protect delicate skin naturally." These are therapeutic claims on cosmetics.
- No origin story. No farm. No reason to trust them beyond "handmade Bangalore."
- Product names are purely aesthetic: Jasmine Cascade, Lavender Field, Wild Rose, Terrazzo. Zero SEO value, zero ingredient transparency.
- No comparison content, no FAQ, no schema markup.

**Keywords they're missing:**
- "goat milk soap Bangalore", "glycerin soap Bangalore" (no base-specific pages)
- "[ingredient] soap India" queries (no ingredient pages)
- "SLS free soap India" (not mentioned anywhere)
- "glycerin vs goat milk soap" (no comparison content)

### 2. Turiyam Soaps

**What they have:**
- Named customer testimonials (good)
- Founder story (genuine differentiation)
- 4 brand pillars (ingredient transparency, clean formulations, small batch, nature)
- Broad product range covering body, face, hair, lip, candles
- YouTube presence

**What they're doing wrong:**
- Too broad. They sell soaps, body butter, hair oil, hair masks, face creams, lip balms, and candles. Topical authority diluted across too many categories — Google won't consider them a soap authority.
- Discount-led: "10% Off On Your First Order" as the primary CTA. Trains buyers to wait for a deal and erodes premium positioning.
- Generic page title: "Buy Natural Handmade Soaps | Turiyam Soaps" — no city, no differentiator.
- Product names mix ingredient-focused ("Activated Charcoal & Bentonite Clay Soap") with completely generic ("Ocean Breeze", "Red Wine Soap") — inconsistent strategy.
- No city-specific content anywhere on the site.
- No comparison pages, no FAQ schema, no ingredient detail pages.
- Testimonials are unnamed cities — "Ms. Abhirami", no location specified. Less credible to Bangalore buyer than named Bangalore reviewers.

**Keywords they're missing:**
- All city-specific queries
- All comparison queries
- "Ayurvedic soap India" (they mention "Indian spices" but no Ayurvedic angle)
- "SLS free soap", "paraben free soap India"

### 3. Paper Boat Collective

**What they have:**

- Physical stores in both cities (strong local SEO signal)

**What they're doing wrong:**
- All 10 soaps on the page are sold out. They rank, but cannot convert. This is the worst possible combination.
- Zero product descriptions. No copy on any soap.
- No ingredient information anywhere.
- Soap is a minor category inside a lifestyle store that also sells women's clothing, men's items, accessories, kids goods, and home products. Google cannot categorise them as a soap authority.
- Product names have zero SEO value: "Kintsugi Japanese Soap", "Mystic Messenger", "Island Breeze." A Bangalore buyer searching "handmade soap Bangalore" finds a sold-out page with no descriptions.
- No reviews, no trust signals, no FAQ, no schema.

**Keywords they're missing:**
- Everything. They're ranking on domain authority from their physical store presence, not from content.

## How Healing Soil beats them

### 1. Comparison cluster — owned (11 pages live)

As of 2026-05-02, GSC showed Healing Soil at positions 5–11 for "glycerin vs goat milk soap", "shea butter vs glycerin", etc. All 11 comparison pages are now live at `/compare/[slug]`. None of the 4 competitors have a single comparison page. The entire cluster is uncontested.

### 2. Mechanism story is a moat nobody can copy

The glycerin story — "commercial soap manufacturers remove glycerin from the bar because glycerin sells for more separately, so you get a drying bar that strips your skin" — is true, specific, and compelling. None of the competitors mention this. It answers the buyer's real question (why does commercial soap leave my skin feeling tight?) and positions Healing Soil's soap as the logical fix. This is live across the home page LP, comparison pages, ingredient pages, and blog posts. No competitor can copy this without copying Healing Soil's exact story.

### 3. Farm origin is unique

Zero competitors have a farm. "Grown on our Goa farm" for neem, tulsi, and other ingredients is a genuine provenance claim nobody else can make. It is embedded in all 14 ingredient pages and all 11 comparison pages.

### 4. Bangalore social proof is sharper — and now on a dedicated page

Turiyam has testimonials without city attribution. Nivre lists a Bangalore address but has no Bangalore-specific content. `/soap/bangalore` is live with Bangalore-specific copy, named reviewers (Lisha, Snehal), a hard water FAQ, and cash-on-delivery confirmation. A buyer searching "handmade soap Bangalore" lands on a page written for them specifically — none of the competitors do this.

### 5. CDSCO compliance is a competitive moat

Nivre uses "calm irritation" and "protect delicate skin." If they ever get flagged, they have to rewrite their positioning. Healing Soil's restrained language (suitable for sensitive skin, gentle lather, no SLS) is legally clean and builds trust with ingredient-reading buyers who are sceptical of overclaiming. The less Healing Soil claims, the more credible the claims it does make.
