Anyone who has dealt with eczema knows the cycle. It calms down. Something triggers it. It flares up again. You spend weeks managing it, and then something as ordinary as changing your soap sets it off.

That last part, the soap, is worth paying attention to.

## What eczema skin is actually dealing with

Eczema is not just dry skin. It is skin whose natural protective barrier is not working properly. Healthy skin keeps moisture in and keeps irritants out. Eczema skin struggles to do both. Moisture leaks out too easily. Irritants get in too easily.

When you wash with a harsh soap, you strip away the protective layer that eczema skin is already struggling to maintain. Most people's skin repairs this within a few hours. For someone with eczema, that repair takes longer and is never quite complete. Each wash with the wrong product sets things back.

## What to avoid

The main culprits are in most commercial soaps and body washes.

**Synthetic foaming agents** (often listed as SLS or SLES) are what make soap foam dramatically. They clean by stripping everything off the skin surface, including the protective fats that eczema skin needs. If a soap produces thick, abundant foam, it almost certainly contains these.

**Synthetic fragrance** is listed simply as "fragrance" or "parfum" on a label. Behind that single word can be dozens of different compounds. It is the most common trigger for skin reactions in cosmetic products, and it is in almost everything.

**Artificial colourants and alcohol-based preservatives** are also worth avoiding. Neither is necessary for effective cleaning, and both are common triggers.

## What actually helps

For eczema-prone skin, the goal is simple: clean without stripping. A natural soap that keeps its glycerin intact (commercial soaps remove it) will moisturise as it cleans. A soap with neem adds natural anti-inflammatory and antibacterial properties, which matters for eczema skin that is also prone to getting infected at scratch sites.

The shorter and more readable the ingredients list, the fewer potential triggers. If you can recognise every ingredient, that is a good sign.

## Managing expectations

Natural soap will not cure eczema. Nothing you put on your skin will. But removing a daily irritant and replacing it with something that supports rather than disrupts the skin barrier can meaningfully reduce the frequency and severity of flares.

It is one less thing working against you.
