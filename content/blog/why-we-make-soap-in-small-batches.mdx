People sometimes ask why our delivery takes a few extra days compared to ordering from a larger brand. It is a fair question, and the answer is straightforward.

We make your soap after you order it.

## How this started

Healing Soil started because Deepanjali, who runs things from our base in South Goa, could not find a soap she was happy putting on her own skin every day. Everything available commercially was either full of things she did not want, or made to be sold in bulk and stored for months before reaching anyone.

She started making soap herself, in small amounts, with ingredients she could account for. That is still how we do it.

## What happens when you scale up

At some point we tried making larger batches and holding stock. It seemed like the practical thing to do.

Two things happened.

The first is that ingredient quality changed. Natural ingredients like neem, turmeric, and dried botanical powders are not the same after they have been sitting for months. The potency fades. You would not notice it dramatically in one bar, but we noticed it in the difference between a fresh batch and one made from older stock.

The second is that we started looking at preservatives. Holding stock means managing shelf life, and natural soap without preservatives has a limited window. We did not want to add preservatives, but without them, holding large quantities of finished soap became a problem.

We chose not to hold stock instead.

## What this means for you

When your order arrives, the soap inside was made in the days before it was packed and shipped. The ingredients are fresh. Nothing has been sitting in a warehouse. There are no preservatives because there is no need for them.

You are not getting the last bar of a batch made three months ago. You are getting something made for your order.

That is the trade-off. A few extra days for a bar that has not been compromised to make logistics easier.

## What goes into our soaps

Depending on the bar, we use neem, tulsi, turmeric, oats, orange peel powder, pomegranate powder, rosemary, rice flour, ginger, vitamins, and essential oils. The bases are glycerin, goat milk, and shea butter, all chosen because they are good for skin rather than good for shelf life.
