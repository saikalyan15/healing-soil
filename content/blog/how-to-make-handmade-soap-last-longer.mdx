One of the first things people notice when they switch to handmade natural soap is that the bars seem to go faster. A few weeks in, half the bar is already gone. With commercial soap, the same bar might last two months.

This is real, and there is a simple reason for it. But with a couple of small changes to how you store and use the bar, you can easily double its life.

## Why natural soap goes faster

Commercial soap bars contain hardening agents and waxes that help them stay firm. They are designed to resist melting, even when they get wet repeatedly. Natural soap does not have any of these additives.

Natural soap also holds onto its glycerin, which is what makes it feel good on your skin. Glycerin is attracted to water. This is good for your skin while you wash, but it means the bar itself absorbs moisture from the air and from sitting in a puddle of water. A bar that stays wet is a bar that dissolves fast.

The fix is almost entirely about keeping the bar dry between uses.

## Get a soap dish with drainage

This single change makes the biggest difference. If your soap sits in standing water between showers, it is dissolving constantly. A soap dish with holes or slats that lets water drain away means the bar can actually dry out between uses.

If your current dish holds water, drill a few holes in the bottom or replace it. It is worth it.

## Keep it out of the shower spray

Many people keep their soap on a shelf inside the shower where the spray hits it directly. Every time you shower, the soap is getting wet for the full duration, not just the moment you use it.

Moving it even a few centimetres out of the direct spray path makes a noticeable difference. Or use a soap bag that hangs and drains freely.

## Rotate between two bars

If you want your soap to last as long as possible, use two bars and switch between them. Each bar gets a full day to dry out completely before its next use. Both bars last significantly longer than either would with daily use.

## Store spare bars away from the bathroom

Bathrooms are humid. A bar sitting in a bathroom cabinet is absorbing moisture even when it is not being used. Keep spare bars in a drawer in another room until you need them.

Here is a bonus: natural soap continues to cure after it is made, and a bar that has been stored dry for a month or two is actually harder and longer-lasting than a fresh bar. Buying ahead pays off.

## Use less than you think you need

Natural soap does not lather the same way commercial soap does. The foam is softer and less dense. New users sometimes keep working up more lather because it does not look like what they are used to.

You need less. A few passes across skin is enough. Once you adjust to this, the bar lasts noticeably longer.

## What to expect

With a draining soap dish and a spot out of direct spray, a natural soap bar should comfortably last three to four weeks with daily use. Some people get longer.

If a bar is disappearing in a week, it is almost always a storage issue, not a soap issue.
